Climate Change and Renewable Energy

Climate change is one of the most important global problems of today, and the transition to renewable energy sources is of critical importance to reduce the effects of this problem. This project examines the relationship between CO2 emissions per capita and the use of renewable energy sources. By analysing data sets related to global CO2 emissions and renewable energy use, our purpose is identifying trends and solutions for promoting sustainable energy practices. With visualizations and data-based insights, we try to provide a extensive overview of the current state of renewable energy use and its effects on decreasing the carbon footprint.

Data Analysis

In order to do this, we took the total amount of electricity production of countries by year and the amount of electricity generated from renewable energy sources. Data visualization techniques were used to present key findings.

Findings

The analysis revealed:

  • A significant increase in global temperatures over the past century.
  • The dominant role of human activities in driving climate change.
  • The growing importance of renewable energy sources in reducing carbon emissions.

Conclusions

The project concludes that:

  • Urgent action is needed to address climate change.
  • Renewable energy plays a crucial role in mitigating climate change.
  • Further research and development in renewable energy technologies are essential.
